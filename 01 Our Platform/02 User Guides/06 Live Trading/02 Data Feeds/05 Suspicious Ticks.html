Tick data is raw and unfiltered, so the data can contain a lot of noise. If a tick is not tradable, we flag it as suspicious. You should avoid using suspicious ticks in your algorithms as informative data points. We flag ticks as suspicious in the following situations:

  • The tick occurs below the best bid quote or above the best ask quote
  • The tick occurs far from the current market price
  • The tick occurs on a dark pool

We only recommend advanced users incorporate their own tick filtering process. You can ignore the suspicious flag and just accept every tick that is injected into your algorithm.
