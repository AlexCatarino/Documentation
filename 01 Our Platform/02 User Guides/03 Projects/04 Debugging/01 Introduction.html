Errors are particularly costly in quant trading software. Some examples of errors are placing incorrect trades, unexpected algorithm crashes, and faulty risk management logic. Developers typically spend a large portion of their time debugging these types of errors. Debugging is the process of diagnosing and resolving errors in your code. Since LEAN is under constant development, you may need to debug your code when breaking changes occur. This page explains what coding errors are and what tools you have available to you to assist with debugging them.
