<h4>Fee model
What is the fee model:
-Fee models set the fees for each order. The quantity and the currency.
-Like all models, they are set on a security-by-security basis
-We provide customized fee models for all brokerages, but you can also set your own.

Why is there a fee model:
-To accurately model the fees/commissions incurred with each order that is filled

<h4>Buying power model
What is the buying power model:
- Buying power models (also known as margin models) control how much buying power (leverage) your algorithm has to make trades.

Why is there a buying power model:
- Buying power calculations can be very complex and depend on many factors, including the brokerage or even time of day.
- To model intraday pattern day trading for US equities (PDT rule)

<h4>Margin call model
What is the margin call model
-The model responsible for picking which orders should be executed during a margin call

Why is there a margin call model:
-To ensure the portfolio stays within margin limits specified by the brokerage.
-To execute synchronous orders to bring the account within margin requirements.
-To liquidate the portfolio immediately before the portfolio gets sub zero if there is a margin call,
-To scan the portfolio and the updated data for a potential margin call situation which may get the holdings below zero

<h4>Settlement model
What is the settlement model:
-The model responsible for applying cash settlement rules
-After a trade has occurred, the brokerage needs to apply the change to the funds in your account

Why is there a settlement model:
-To apply cash settlement rules
-The most common settlement type is immediate - where the funds are available for trading immediately.
-US Equities trading with cash accounts is typically settled 3 days after the transaction occurred.

<h4>Short availability model
What is the short availability model:
-A model that tracks the number of shares that are available to be borrowed for shorting

Why is there a short availability model:
-If you have a trading strategy that shorts, you'll need to know if there are shares available to short.
-Without a short availability model, your backtest results can be unrealistic. If, for example, you're shorting penny stocks that don't actually have shares available to short at the time of your trade.
-To ensure your backtest short trades are realistic. -- If the performance can continue in live trading.
