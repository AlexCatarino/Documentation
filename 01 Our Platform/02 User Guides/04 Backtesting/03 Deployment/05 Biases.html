What are backtesting biases:
  - Mistakes that can make backtest results look very good but the results won't continue into live trading

Why are there biases:
   - They are a result of the nature of backtesting
   - Naive researching / unexperienced

What's the result of backtesting with these biases:
  - There is an opportunity cost of researching/deploying strategies with the following biases (lost time)
  - They may cost you money in live trading

<h4>Overfitting
  - Caused by tweaking parameters or using the optimizer
  - Modeled too closely to the past, may not translate well to unseen data
  - To avoid, use few parameters (simpler is usually better)
  - Avoid running too many backtests for a single strategy/hypothesis
 

<h4>Lookahead bias
   - Caused by using information in the future to make decisions in the present
   - Avoided by using the backtesting environment since it's event driven and you can't pass the time frontier
   - By running multiple backtests, you effectively has lookahead bias seep into your research

<h4>Survivorship bias
    - Our datasets are free of survivorship bias
         - Even if a security was delisted, it's included in backtest simulations
    - It's best to you use a dynamic universe instead of selecting a manual universe with securities that you know have performed well
