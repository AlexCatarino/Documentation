What are optimization constraints:
- "Optimization Constraints filter out backtests from your results which do not conform to your desired range of statistical results." // IDE definition

Why use optimization constraints:
- Optimization constraints are optional
- If you add constraints, the results of your optimization won't include backtests that don't perform within your constraints
- It's a way to incorporate the result of mulitple objective functions into a single optimization

What constraints can I use:
-You can add any number of constraints
-Constraints consist of a target (Sharpe, PSR, etc), an operator (<, <=, ==, !=, >, >=), and a numerical value
