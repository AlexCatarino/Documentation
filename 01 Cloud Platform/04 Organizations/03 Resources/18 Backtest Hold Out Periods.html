To reduce the chance of overfitting, organization managers can enforce all backtests must end a certain number of months before the current date. For example, if you set a one year hold out period, the researchers on your team will not be able to use the most recent year of data in their backtests. A hold out period is helpful because it leaves you an out-of-sample period to test your model after your done the development stage. To change the backtest hold out period, open the organization homepage and then scroll down to the Backtesting Out of Sample Period section and enter the out-of-sample period duration.
