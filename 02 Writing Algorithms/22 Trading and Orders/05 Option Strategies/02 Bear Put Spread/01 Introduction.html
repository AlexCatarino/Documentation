Bear put spread, which is also known as short put spread, consists of buying an ITM put and selling an OTM put. Both puts have the same underlying stock and the same expiration date. The OTM put serve as a hedge of the ITM put.

It is a option strategy profitting from a decline in underlying asset price. This is a limited-reward-limited-risk strategy.

$Payout_{OTM\ put}=(Strike_{OTM\ put}-Price_{underlying})^{+}$

$Payout_{ITM\ put}=(Strike_{ITM\ put}-Price_{underlying})^{+}$

$Profit/Loss=(Payout_{ITM\ put}-Payout_{OTM\ put}+credit\ received_{OTM\ put}-debit\ paid_{ITM\ put})\times multiplier-commissions$ 

price = np.arange(750,1150,1)
k_low = 900 # lower strike price for put
k_high = 1000 # higher strike price for put
price_low = 20 # premium of put option with lower strike
price_high = 70 # premium of put option with higher strike

# short put with higher strike
payoff_short_put = [min(price_low, -(k_low-i-price_low)) for i in price]
# long put with lower strike
payoff_long_put = [max(-price_high, k_high-i-price_high) for i in price]
payoff = np.sum([payoff_long_put, payoff_short_put], axis=0)

plt.figure(figsize=(20,11))
plt.plot(price, payoff_long_put, label = 'long put')
plt.plot(price, payoff_short_put, label = 'short put')
plt.plot(price, payoff, label = 'Bear Put Spread')
plt.legend(fontsize = 20)
plt.xlabel('Stock Price at Expiry',fontsize = 15)
plt.ylabel('payoff',fontsize = 15)
plt.title('Bear Put Spread Payoff at Expiration',fontsize = 20)
plt.grid(True)

The maximum profit is the spread between the 2 options' strike prices minus net debit paid when opening the trade. If the price is declined and lower than the strike prices of both puts, they will worth $(strike - underlying price) at expiration.

Maximum loss is the net debit paid after commission when opening the trade, where is underlying price is above both strike prices and the options become worthless.

If the option is American option, there will be a risk of early assignment on the shorted option.
