-When you place a buy (sell) LIT order, set the trigger price below (above) the current market price.

To send a limit if touched order, you must provide a symbol, quantity, trigger price, and limit price. If you do not have sufficient capital for the order, it is rejected.

// Once Bitcoin trades down to $40,000, place a limit order to buy 1 Bitcoin at $38,000
var unmarketableBuyTicket = LimitIfTouchedOrder("BTCUSD", 1, 40000, 38000);

// Once Bitcoin trades down to $40,000, place a limit order to buy 1 Bitcoin at $45,000
var marketableBuyTicket = LimitIfTouchedOrder("BTCUSD", 1, 40000, 45000);

// Once Bitcoin trades up to $100,000, place a limit order to sell 1 Bitcoin at $105,000
var unmarketableSellTicket = LimitIfTouchedOrder("BTCUSD", -1, 100000, 105000);

// Once Bitcoin trades up to $100,000, place a limit order to sell 1 Bitcoin at $95,000
var marketableSellTicket = LimitIfTouchedOrder("BTCUSD", -1, 100000, 95000);
# Once Bitcoin trades down to $40,000, place a limit order to buy 1 Bitcoin at $38,000
unmarketable_buy_ticket = self.LimitIfTouchedOrder("BTCUSD", 1, 40000, 38000)

# Once Bitcoin trades down to $40,000, place a limit order to buy 1 Bitcoin at $45,000
marketable_buy_ticket = self.LimitIfTouchedOrder("BTCUSD", 1, 40000, 45000)

# Once Bitcoin trades up to $100,000, place a limit order to sell 1 Bitcoin at $105,000
unmarketable_sell_ticket = self.LimitIfTouchedOrder("BTCUSD", -1, 100000, 105000)

# Once Bitcoin trades up to $100,000, place a limit order to sell 1 Bitcoin at $95,000
marketable_sell_ticket = self.LimitIfTouchedOrder("BTCUSD", -1, 100000, 95000)

You can provide a tag and order properties to the LimitIfTouchedOrder method.

LimitIfTouchedOrder(symbol, quantity, triggerPrice, limitPrice, tag, orderProperties);
self.LimitIfTouchedOrder(symbol, quantity, trigger_price, limit_price, tag, order_properties)
