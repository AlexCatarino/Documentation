Bear call spread, also known as short call spread, consists of buying an OTM call and selling an ITM call. Both calls have the same underlying Equity and the same expiration date. The OTM call serves as a hedge for the ITM call. The bear call spread profits from a decline in underlying asset price and the time decay value of the ITM call.

The bear call spread is a limited-reward-limited-risk strategy. The payoff is

$Payoff_{OTM\ call}=(Price_{underlying}-Strike_{OTM\ call})^{+}$

$Payoff_{ITM\ call}=(Price_{underlying}-Strike_{ITM\ call})^{+}$

$Profit/Loss=(Payoff_{OTM\ call}-Payoff_{ITM\ call}+credit\ received_{ITM\ call}-debit\ paid_{OTM\ call})\times multiplier-commissions$

The maximum profit is the net credit received after commission when opening the trade. If the price declines, both calls expire worthless.

The maximum loss is the spread between the strike prices of the 2 contracts minus the net credit received when opening the trade, where is underlying price is above both strike prices and the options being exercised/assigned.

If the Option is an American Option, there is a risk of early assignment on the sold contract.
