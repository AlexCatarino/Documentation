Bull call spread, also known as long call spread, consists of buying an ITM call and selling an OTM call. Both calls have the same underlying Equity and the same expiration date. The OTM call serves as a hedge for the ITM call. The bull call spread profits from a rise in underlying asset price.

The bull call spread is a limited-reward-limited-risk strategy. The payoff is

$Payoff_{OTM\ call}=(Price_{underlying}-Strike_{OTM\ call})^{+}$

$Payoff_{ITM\ call}=(Price_{underlying}-Strike_{ITM\ call})^{+}$

$Profit/Loss=(Payoff_{ITM\ call}-Payoff_{OTM\ call}+credit\ received_{OTM\ call}-debit\ paid_{ITM\ call})\times multiplier-commissions$

The maximum profit is the spread between the strike prices of the 2 contracts minus net debit paid when opening the trade after commission. If the underlying price increases to exceed both strikes at expiration, both calls are worth $(underlying price - strike).

The maximum loss is the net debit paid after commission when opening the trade, where is underlying price is below both strike prices and the options become worthless.

If the Option is an American Option, there is a risk of early assignment on the sold contract.
