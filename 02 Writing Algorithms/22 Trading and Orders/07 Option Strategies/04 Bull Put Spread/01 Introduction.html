Bull put spread, also known as long put spread, consists of buying an OTM put and selling an ITM put. Both puts have the same underlying Equity and the same expiration date. The OTM put serve as a hedge for the ITM put. The bull put spread profits from a rise in underlying asset price.

This is a limited-reward-limited-risk strategy. The payoff is

$$ \begin{array}{rcll} P^{OTM}_T & = & (K^{OTM} - S_T)^{+}\\ P^{ITM}_T & = & (K^{ITM} - S_T)^{+}\\ P_T & = & (P^{OTM}_T - P^{ITM}_T + P^{ITM}_0 - P^{OTM}_0)\times m - fee\\ \end{array} $$ $$ \begin{array}{rcll} \textrm{where} & P^{OTM}_T & = & \textrm{OTM put value at time T}\\ & P^{ITM}_T & = & \textrm{ITM put value at time T}\\ & S_T & = & \textrm{Underlying asset price at time T}\\ & K^{OTM} & = & \textrm{OTM put strike price}\\ & K^{ITM} & = & \textrm{ITM put strike price}\\ & P_T & = & \textrm{Payout total at time T}\\ & P^{ITM}_0 & = & \textrm{ITM put value at position opening (credit received)}\\ & P^{OTM}_0 & = & \textrm{OTM put value at position opening (debit paid)}\\ & m & = & \textrm{Contract multiplier}\\ & T & = & \textrm{Time of expiration} \end{array} $$

The maximum profit is the net credit received: $P^{ITM}_0 - P^{OTM}_0$. If the price rised to higher than the strike prices of both puts, both puts become worthless.

Maximum loss is $K^{ITM} - K^{OTM} + P^{ITM}_0 - P^{OTM}_0$, where the underlying price is below both strike prices.

If the Option is American Option, there will be a risk of early assignment on the shorted Option.
