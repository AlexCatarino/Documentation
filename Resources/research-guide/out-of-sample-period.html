To reduce the chance of overfitting, organization managers can enforce all backtests must end a certain number of months before the current date. For example, if you set a one year out-of-sample period, the researchers on your team will not be able to use the most recent year of data in their backtests. A out-of-sample period is helpful because it leaves you a period to test your model after your done the development stage. To change the backtest out-of-sample period, open the organization homepage and then scroll down to the Backtesting Out of Sample Period section and enter the out-of-sample period duration.
