You must import the plotting packages and get some historical data to create pie charts.

In this example, you create a pie chart that shows the weights of the banking securities if you scale the weights based on the inverse volatility of each security. Follow these steps to create the pie chart:

  1. Select the close column and then call the unstack method.
    2. close_prices = history['close'].unstack(level=0)
  2. Call the pct_change method.
    3. daily_returns = close_prices.pct_change()
  3. Call the var method and then take the inverse.
    4. inverse_variance = 1 / daily_ret.var()
  4. Convert the Series into a DataFrame and then call the reset_index method.
    5. inverse_variance = pd.DataFrame(inverse_variance, columns=["inverse variance"]).reset_index()
  5. Call the pie method with inverse_variance, the column name of the values, and the column name of the names.
    6. fig = px.pie(inverse_variance, values='inverse variance', names='symbol')
  6. Call the update_layout method with a title.
    7. fig.update_layout(title='Asset Allocation of bank stocks and bank sector ETF');
  7. Show the Figure.
    8. fig.show()
