QuantConnect recommends developing an algorithmic trading strategy based on a central hypothesis. An algorithm hypothesis should be developed at the start of your research, and the remaining time spent exploring how to test your theory. If you find yourself deviating from your core theory, or introducing code which isn't based around that hypothesis, you should stop and go back to thesis development.

Hypothesis development is somewhat of an art, and requires creativity and great observation skills, yet is the most powerful skill a quant can learn. We recommend an algorithm hypothesis follows the pattern of cause and effect. Your aim should be to express your strategy in the following sentence:

A change in {cause} leads to an {effect}.

You can search for inspiration of great causes from your own training and experience, media or intuition. Generally causes of financial market movements fall into three categories; Human psychology, real world events/fundamentals or invisible financial actions.
